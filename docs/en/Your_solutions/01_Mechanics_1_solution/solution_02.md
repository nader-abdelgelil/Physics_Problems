# Range Optimization – Projectile Motion

## Problem
Show analytically that the maximum range:

R(θ) = (v0² sin(2θ)) / g

is achieved at θ = 45°

---

## Step 1: Treat constants

Let:

K = v0² / g

So:

R(θ) = K sin(2θ)

---

## Step 2: Differentiate

dR/dθ = K * d/dθ [sin(2θ)]

Using chain rule:

dR/dθ = K * 2cos(2θ)

---

## Step 3: Find critical points

Set derivative = 0:

2K cos(2θ) = 0

cos(2θ) = 0

---

## Step 4: Solve

2θ = 90°  
θ = 45°

---

## Step 5: Verify maximum

Second derivative:

d²R/dθ² = -4K sin(2θ)

At θ = 45°:

sin(90°) = 1

d²R/dθ² = -4K < 0 → maximum

---

## Final Answer

The range is maximized at:

θ = 45°
