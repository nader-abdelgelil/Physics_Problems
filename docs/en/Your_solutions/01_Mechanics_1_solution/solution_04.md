# Vector Calculus – Velocity & Acceleration

## Problem
Given the position vector:

r(t) = (3t²)i + (5t - 8t²)j

Find:
- Velocity vector v(t)
- Acceleration vector a(t)

---

## Step 1: Velocity = first derivative

v(t) = dr/dt

Differentiate each component:

x(t) = 3t² → dx/dt = 6t  
y(t) = 5t - 8t² → dy/dt = 5 - 16t  

So:

v(t) = (6t)i + (5 - 16t)j

---

## Step 2: Acceleration = second derivative

a(t) = dv/dt

Differentiate again:

dx/dt = 6t → d²x/dt² = 6  
dy/dt = 5 - 16t → d²y/dt² = -16  

So:

a(t) = 6i - 16j

---

## Final Answer

Velocity:
v(t) = (6t)i + (5 - 16t)j

Acceleration:
a(t) = 6i - 16j
