# Relative Velocity – Boat & River

## Problem
- River velocity: 2 m/s east
- Boat speed (in still water): 5 m/s
- River width: 200 m

Find:
1. Direction to head
2. Time to cross

---

## Step 1: Define goal

Boat wants to go **directly north** relative to ground.

So:
Net east velocity = 0

---

## Step 2: Cancel river drift

Let θ = angle west of north

Boat horizontal component must cancel river:

5 sin(θ) = 2

sin(θ) = 2/5

θ ≈ 23.6°

---

## Step 3: Vertical velocity

North component:

v_y = 5 cos(θ)

cos(θ) = √(1 - (2/5)²) = √(21/25)

v_y = 5 × √21 / 5 = √21 ≈ 4.58 m/s

---

## Step 4: Time to cross

Time = Distance / Speed

t = 200 / 4.58 ≈ 43.7 seconds

---

## Final Answer

- Direction: ≈ 23.6° west of north  
- Time to cross: ≈ 43.7 seconds
