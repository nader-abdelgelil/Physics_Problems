# Elimination of Time & Motion Analysis

## Problem
Given:
x(t) = 2t²  
y(t) = 3t³  

Find:
- Cartesian equation
- Velocity & acceleration
- Magnitudes
- Check if acceleration is constant

---

## Step 1: Eliminate parameter t

From x(t):

t² = x / 2  
t = √(x/2)

Substitute into y:

y = 3t³ = 3(t)(t²)

y = 3 √(x/2) × (x/2)

y = (3x/2) √(x/2)

---

## Final Cartesian Equation

y = (3x/2) √(x/2)

---

## Step 2: Velocity

v(t) = dr/dt

x(t) = 2t² → dx/dt = 4t  
y(t) = 3t³ → dy/dt = 9t²  

v(t) = (4t)i + (9t²)j

---

## Step 3: Speed (magnitude)

|v(t)| = √[(4t)² + (9t²)²]

= √(16t² + 81t⁴)

= t √(16 + 81t²)

---

## Step 4: Acceleration

a(t) = dv/dt

dx/dt = 4t → d²x/dt² = 4  
dy/dt = 9t² → d²y/dt² = 18t  

a(t) = 4i + (18t)j

---

## Step 5: Acceleration magnitude

|a(t)| = √(4² + (18t)²)

= √(16 + 324t²)

---

## Step 6: Is acceleration constant?

a(t) = (4, 18t)

- x-component is constant  
- y-component depends on t  

➡️ Acceleration is NOT constant

---

## Final Answers

- Trajectory: y = (3x/2)√(x/2)  
- Velocity: v(t) = (4t)i + (9t²)j  
- Speed: |v(t)| = t√(16 + 81t²)  
- Acceleration: a(t) = 4i + (18t)j  
- Acceleration is NOT constant
