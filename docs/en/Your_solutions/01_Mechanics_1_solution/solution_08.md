# Circular Motion – Earth Rotation

## Problem
Find the centripetal acceleration at the Earth's equator.

Given:
- Radius R = 6378 km = 6.378 × 10^6 m
- Earth completes 1 rotation in 24 hours = 86400 s

---

## Step 1: Angular velocity

ω = 2π / T

ω = 2π / 86400 ≈ 7.27 × 10^-5 rad/s

---

## Step 2: Centripetal acceleration

a_c = ω² R

a_c = (7.27 × 10^-5)² × (6.378 × 10^6)

---

## Step 3: Calculate

ω² ≈ 5.29 × 10^-9

a_c ≈ 5.29 × 10^-9 × 6.378 × 10^6

a_c ≈ 0.034 m/s²

---

## Final Answer

Centripetal acceleration ≈ 0.034 m/s²
