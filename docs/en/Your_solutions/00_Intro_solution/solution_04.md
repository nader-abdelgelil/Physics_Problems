# Solution 04: Rearranging Formulas (The Pendulum)

## 1. Problem Statement
The formula for the period of a simple pendulum is:
$$T = 2\pi \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$
Rearrange the equation to isolate **$g$** (acceleration due to gravity).

---

## 2. Step-by-Step Derivation

### Step 1: Divide by $2\pi$
Isolate the square root term by dividing both sides by $2\pi$:
$$\frac{T}{2\pi} = \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}}$$

### Step 2: Square both sides
To remove the square root (radical), square both sides of the equation:
$$\left( \frac{T}{2\pi} \right)^2 = \left( \sqrt{\frac{L}{g}} \right)^2$$
$$\frac{T^2}{4\pi^2} = \frac{L}{g}$$

### Step 3: Use the reciprocal (Flip)
Flip both fractions to move $g$ to the numerator:
$$\frac{4\pi^2}{T^2} = \frac{g}{L}$$

### Step 4: Isolate $g$
Multiply both sides by $L$ to complete the isolation:
$$g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}$$

---

## 3. Final Formula
The acceleration due to gravity can be calculated using:
$$\mathbf{g = \frac{4\pi^2 L}{T^2}}$$

---

## 4. Dimensional Analysis (Verification)
- **Length ($L$):** meters ($m$)
- **Period ($T^2$):** seconds squared ($s^2$)
- **Resulting Units:** $m/s^2$
Since $m/s^2$ is the standard unit for acceleration, the rearranged formula is dimensionally correct.
