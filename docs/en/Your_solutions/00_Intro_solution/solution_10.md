# Solution 10: Free Fall (Equations of Motion)

## 1. Problem Statement
An object is dropped from the top of a building and takes **3 seconds** to hit the ground. 
- **Calculate**: The height of the building.
- **Assume**: Acceleration due to gravity $g = 9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$ and ignore air resistance.

---

## 2. Given Data
- **Initial Velocity ($v_i$ or $u$)**: $0 \, \text{m/s}$ (Since it was "dropped").
- **Time ($t$)**: $3 \, \text{s}$.
- **Gravity ($g$)**: $9.8 \, \text{m/s}^2$.
- **Height ($h$ or $d$)**: ? (Required).

---

## 3. The Formula
We use the second equation of motion for constant acceleration:
$$d = v_i t + \frac{1}{2} g t^2$$

---

## 4. Step-by-Step Calculation

### Step 1: Substitute the values
Since $v_i = 0$, the first part of the equation ($v_i t$) becomes zero:
$$h = 0 \times (3) + \frac{1}{2} \times (9.8) \times (3)^2$$

### Step 2: Simplify the expression
$$h = \frac{1}{2} \times 9.8 \times 9$$
$$h = 4.9 \times 9$$

### Step 3: Final Calculation
$$\mathbf{h = 44.1 \, \text{meters}}$$

---
