# Solution 07: Significant Figures & Scientific Notation

## 1. Problem Statement
Perform the following calculation and express the answer with the correct number of **significant figures** and in **scientific notation**:
$$(2.50 \times 10^4) \times (6.0 \times 10^3)$$

---

## 2. Mathematical Steps

### Step 1: Multiply the Numbers (Coefficients)
Multiply $2.50$ by $6.0$:
$$2.50 \times 6.0 = 15.00$$

### Step 2: Multiply the Powers of 10
When multiplying powers of 10, we **add** the exponents:
$$10^4 \times 10^3 = 10^{4+3} = 10^7$$

### Step 3: Combine the Results
$$15.00 \times 10^7$$

### Step 4: Apply Scientific Notation Rules
In scientific notation, the decimal point must be after the first non-zero digit:
$$15.00 \times 10^7 = 1.500 \times 10^8$$

---

## 3. Applying Significant Figures (The Final Rule)
- **$2.50$** has **3** significant figures.
- **$6.0$** has **2** significant figures.
- **Rule**: When multiplying, the final answer must match the measurement with the **least** number of significant figures.

Therefore, we must round **$1.500 \times 10^8$** to **2** significant figures.

**Final Answer:**
$$\mathbf{1.5 \times 10^8}$$

---

## 4. Summary of Rules
1. **Non-zero digits** are always significant.
2. **Trailing zeros** after a decimal point are significant (e.g., the `.0` in $6.0$).
3. **Scientific Notation Form**: $a \times 10^n$, where $1 \le a < 10$.
