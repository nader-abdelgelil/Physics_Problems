# Solution 03: Proportionality (Universal Law of Gravitation)

## 1. Problem Statement
Consider Newton's Universal Law of Gravitation:
$$F = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

We need to determine the factor by which the force $F$ changes if:
- The distance $r$ is **doubled** ($r \to 2r$).
- Both masses $m_1$ and $m_2$ are **halved** ($m_1 \to \frac{1}{2}m_1$ and $m_2 \to \frac{1}{2}m_2$).

---

## 2. Step-by-Step Derivation

### Step 1: Define the Initial State
Let the initial force be $F_1$:
$$F_1 = G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$

### Step 2: Define the New Variables
After applying the changes:
- New distance: $r_{new} = 2r$
- New mass 1: $m_{1(new)} = \frac{1}{2}m_1$
- New mass 2: $m_{2(new)} = \frac{1}{2}m_2$

### Step 3: Substitute into the Formula
Plug the new values into the original equation to find $F_2$:
$$F_2 = G \frac{(\frac{1}{2}m_1) (\frac{1}{2}m_2)}{(2r)^2}$$

### Step 4: Simplify the Expression
1. **Simplify the Numerator**: $(\frac{1}{2} \times \frac{1}{2}) = \frac{1}{4}$
2. **Simplify the Denominator**: $(2r)^2 = 4r^2$

Substitute these back:
$$F_2 = G \frac{\frac{1}{4} m_1 m_2}{4r^2}$$

### Step 5: Extract the Constant Factor
Separate the numerical coefficients from the original variables:
$$F_2 = \left( \frac{1/4}{4} \right) \times G \frac{m_1 m_2}{r^2}$$
$$F_2 = \left( \frac{1}{4} \times \frac{1}{4} \right) \times F_1$$
$$F_2 = \frac{1}{16} F_1$$

---

## 3. Final Conclusion
The force changes by a factor of **$1/16$**. 

**Explanation**: 
- Halving both masses reduces the force to **one-fourth** (Direct proportionality).
- Doubling the distance reduces the force to **one-fourth** (Inverse square law).
- Combined: $\frac{1}{4} \times \frac{1}{4} = \frac{1}{16}$.
