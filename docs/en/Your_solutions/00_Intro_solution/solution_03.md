# Proportionality – Universal Law of Gravitation

## Problem
Given the formula:

F = G * (m1 * m2) / r^2

Determine how the force F changes if:
- Distance r is doubled
- Both masses m1 and m2 are halved

---

## Step 1: Define the original force

F = G * (m1 * m2) / r^2

---

## Step 2: Apply the changes

New values:
- m1 → m1/2
- m2 → m2/2
- r → 2r

---

## Step 3: Substitute into the formula

F_new = G * ((m1/2)(m2/2)) / (2r)^2

---

## Step 4: Simplify step by step

Numerator:
(m1/2)(m2/2) = (m1 * m2) / 4

Denominator:
(2r)^2 = 4r^2

---

## Step 5: Combine everything

F_new = G * (m1 * m2 / 4) / (4r^2)

F_new = G * (m1 * m2) / (16r^2)

---

## Step 6: Compare with original formula

Original:
F = G * (m1 * m2) / r^2

So:

F_new = (1/16) * F

---

## Final Answer

The gravitational force becomes:

F_new = F / 16

➡️ The force decreases by a factor of 16.
