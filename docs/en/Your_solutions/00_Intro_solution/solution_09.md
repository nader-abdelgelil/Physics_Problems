# Optimization Problem – Maximum Area Rectangle

## Problem
A rectangle is under the curve:

y = 3 - x^2

in the first quadrant. Find the dimensions that maximize its area.

---

## Step 1: Define variables

Let:
- width = x
- height = y = 3 - x^2

So, area:

A(x) = x(3 - x^2)

---

## Step 2: Simplify

A(x) = 3x - x^3

---

## Step 3: Differentiate

A'(x) = 3 - 3x^2

---

## Step 4: Find critical points

Set derivative = 0:

3 - 3x^2 = 0  
x^2 = 1  
x = 1  (first quadrant → positive only)

---

## Step 5: Find height

y = 3 - (1)^2 = 2

---

## Step 6: Maximum area

A = 1 × 2 = 2

---

## Final Answer

- Width = 1  
- Height = 2  
- Maximum Area = 2
